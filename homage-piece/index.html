We Are Who We Are: Luca Guadagnino in Conversation

Director Luca Guadagnino discusses transformation and the poetry and potency of his latest coming-of-age HBO series We Are Who We Are, in conversation with Jefferson Hack. // 4.15pm - 5.00pm

Between Genres: What’s Next in Short-Form Video Culture

Join genre-defying rising stars director and photographer Leonn Ward, artist and filmmaker Bafic and director Iggy London as they sit down with NOWNESS video commissioner and Sundance programmer Katie Metcalfe to discuss how to use short film to elevate the unexpected and create a powerful voice that cuts through the noise. // 11.00am - 11.45am

New Perspectives on Fashion Film

Artist, filmmaker and photographer Matt Lambert, dancer and fashion designer Saul Nash and fashion designer Priya Ahluwalia join NOWNESS video commissioner Shelley Jones to explore what makes a fashion film today. Sharing their stories and their work this panel of interdisciplinary creators will discuss how fashion film has become an all-encompassing genre spanning art, design, music and more. // 12.15pm - 1.00pm

Broadcasts From Tomorrow: The Future of Fashion Film

Join Dazed Media and NOWNESS for a special webinar exploring the future of fashion film, branded storytelling and culture in motion. From the birth of a new genre to branded content, this talk explores the movers and shakers behind fashion film, why it is that today the fashion industry cannot exist without video, how Gen Z are consuming video - and finally, what’s next for fashion film in 2020 and beyond. // 2.00pm - 2.45pm

Imagined Universes, Fantasy Futures and Virtual Realities in Film

NOWNESS Commissioner and Sundance Programmer Katie Metcalfe sits down with Bjork’s longstanding collaborator Andrew Thomas Huang and 2020 BAFTA Breakthrough Talent, cinematographer Rina Yang to explore the role cinematography plays in their imagined worlds; the fusion of live action and visual effects, the art of dream collaborations and whether escapism and utopia can affect social change? // 3.15pm - 4.00pm

Moving Images: Dance in Digital Culture

Multidisciplinary artists, choreographers and dancers Ivan Michael Blackstock and Holly Blakey join Creative Director of NOWNESS Bunny Kinney to explore the use of movement as part of multifaceted creative expression, and the rise of dance culture during a year in which art spaces and nightclubs have been closed. // 5.15pm - 6.00pm

Doug Aitken Presents Flags: Post-Covid Art

Artist Doug Aitken presents Flags, a new body of work that he has been developing over the last six months. The online premiere will be followed by a short conversation with Jefferson Hack. Flags is a series of handmade wall hanging works, forms created out of fabric cut from the artist's clothes and collaged into words, sentences and abstractions. The works explore language that create a new topography of ideas of the future. // 6.30pm - 7.15pm

NOWNESS 10 Birthday Screening

A special screening featuring Nowness all-time classics selected by Kate Moss, Gus Van Sant, Honey Dijon, Harris Reed, Kelsey Lu, Simone Rocha, Benjamin Alexander Huseby & Serhat Isik from GmbH, Wilson Oryema, Hans Ulrich Obrist, Beth Ditto and Molly Goddard. // 7.30pm - 8.30pm

Speakers

Luca Guadagnino

Luca Guadagnino is an Italian director, screenwriter and producer known for his visually arresting and emotive style, and for the Academy Award-winning film Call Me by Your Name. After studying literature and cinema history in Rome, he made his directorial debut in 1999 with The Protagonists, starring Tilda Swinton who went on to become a longterm collaborator of his. This year saw the release of his first TV series, a coming-of-age drama called We Are Who We Are.

Leonn Ward

Leonn Ward is an Irish director and photographer whose ability to capture vibrant intimacy is at the heart of her storytelling. She has directed music videos and commercials for the likes of Vogue, Givenchy and Nike, directed equality campaigns for Google, and branded campaigns promoting body confidence for Dior. In 2019, Leonn directed You Ain’t The Problem, the first video to be taken from Michael Kiwanuka’s 2020 Mercury Prize winning album Kiwanuka.

Priya Ahluwalia

Ahluwalia was founded by Ahluwalia in 2018 after graduating from the MA Menswear course at The University of Westminster. In 2020, Ahluwalia won the LVMH Prize and was listed in Forbes' 30 under 30 list. Priya combines elements from her dual Indian-Nigerian heritage and London roots to explore the potential of vintage and deadstock clothing. Priya has also published two books that capture her world through a mix of photographs and personal stories. (credit: Laurence Ellis)

Bafic

Bafic is a director and artist living in London whose work blurs the lines between video art and documentary. Working primarily across the mediums of photography, film, and public installation, Bafic’s work aims to discuss the systems created by people, using his own reflections on 21st-century life and new forms of expression. Bafic's collaborators and clients include Gucci, Louis Vuitton, Vice, Nike, adidas, Sonos, Dazed, Neneh Cherry Mabel McVey, Smirnoff, Boiler Room, ICA, and Tate.

Andrew Thomas Huang

Writer, director and visual artist Andrew Thomas Huang crafts hybrid fantasy worlds and mythical dreamscapes. A Grammy-nominated music video director, Huang's collaborators include Bjork, FKA Twigs, Perfume Genius and Thom Yorke. Inspired by his Chinese heritage, queer Asian mythology and folklore, Huang is in late development on his first feature film Tiger Girl. Huang graduated with a degree in Fine Art and Animation from the University of Southern California.

Matt Lambert

Matt Lambert is a Berlin-based filmmaker, photographer and creative director whose fashion collaborations include Rick Owens, Calvin Klein and Gucci to name a few. He is a longtime contributor to Dazed and NOWNESS, with his film Sweat being the most viewed NOWNESS film of all time. He is also the artistic director of Singular Arts Group — an artist management company representing Mykki Blanco, Patrick Wolf and Finn Ronsdorf — as well as the co-founder of queer, xxx content studio Vitium.

Ivan Michael Blackstock

Cultural innovator Ivan Michael Blackstock is a south London-born multidisciplinary artist who established a successful commercial career working as a dancer and choreographer. Ivan has presented work at the Roundhouse, National Theatre and The Young Vic. Ivan is also the founder of CRXSS PLATFXRM, an organization that spotlights youth and street culture. Most recently, Ivan was selected as a choreographer for Beyonce's Black is King.

Holly Blakey

Redefining the role of contemporary choreographer is Blakey’s calling card. She has a personalized vision and creative power that injects a kinetic sensuality into both her live performances and editorial projects. Working across dance, fashion and music, recent choreographic credits include projects for Gucci, Dior, and Paul Smith, as well as videos for Coldplay, Young Fathers, and Florence and the Machine.

Saul Nash

British designer and choreographer Saul Nash creates contemporary technical garments that are cut and designed for the liberation of movement. An understanding of the complexities of masculinity is informed through Nash’s background as a self-taught dancer and choreographer. Grounded in the ethos of community and embracing his London roots, he works with dancers who invigorate an essence of family while delving deeper into the relationship between performance and menswear.

Iggy London

Iggy London is an award-winning British filmmaker, artist and writer, whose films touch on themes of identity, community and coming of age. He is committed to creating work that shifts the boundaries of cinema and culture. His debut short Black Boys Don’t Cry received widespread attention in 2016. In 2018 he released Velvet, which won Best Experimental Film at the Thunderdance Cannes Film Festival. His most recent short, Rite of Passage, explores masculinity in inner city London.

Hosts

Jefferson Hack

Jefferson Hack is a creative director, curator and co-founder of Dazed Media, which comprises print, digital and video brands AnOther, Dazed, Dazed China, Dazed Korea, Dazed Beauty, Dazed Studio and NOWNESS. Hack has curated events such as the Dazed 20th anniversary photographic exhibition at Somerset House, YOHOOD festival in Shanghai, and Transformer: A Rebirth of Wonder at 180 The Strand. Hack has edited multiple books with Phaidon Press, Steidl and Rizzoli.

Bunny Kinney

Bunny Kinney is the Editor-in-Chief of Dazed Beauty and Creative Director of NOWNESS. Prior to joining Dazed Media in November 2017, Bunny was at i-D from 2014 onwards, where he was appointed the role of Commercial Creative Director in 2015 and led special projects with brands including Gucci, Marc Jacobs, and Chanel. He has also worked regularly as a freelance film-maker, creative director and digital consultant for clients including Dior, MAC Cosmetics, and L’Oreal Luxe.

Shelley Jones

Shelley Jones is Video Commissioner at NOWNESS. As part of the commissioning team, she develops new series and commissions original films across a range of subjects – from art and fashion to design and dance – searching out incredible directorial talent and working with global brands to tell creative stories in short film form. She frequently sits on juries for film festivals and fashion film festivals around the world and engages in panels and discussions regarding the culture of online video.

Katie Metcalfe

Katie joined global video platform NOWNESS as a Video Commissioner in 2016 where she has commissioned editorial films and branded content across fashion, art and culture. She has also been a short film programer for Sundance Film Festival since 2010 and has over 15 years' experience tracking new talent and developing new directorial voices.
